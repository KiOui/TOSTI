How to run a shift

The full baker's guide, start to finish.


1. Log in

Sign in with your Radboud account. You can only manage shifts in venues you have permissions for — if a venue you expect doesn't show up, that's why.


2. Start or join a shift

From the home page, either start a new shift or join one that's already running. Two shifts can't run in the same venue at the same time.

Starting a new shift: pick a start and end time, hit create, and it shows up on the home page. Joining an existing one: click Join shift.

Either way you land on the baker view: a live queue of orders plus a footer with the quick actions you'll use most often (capacity, time, payments, etc.).


3. Open the shift for orders

A new shift doesn't accept orders until you flip it open. Use the button in the footer to toggle. Until you do, anyone trying to order sees a "not currently possible" message.

The shift's max capacity and end-time still apply even after you open it — opening just means you're accepting orders within those limits.


4. Wait for orders

Orders pop into the queue automatically — no refreshing needed. Your own orders jump to the top; use the button in the footer to switch to the user order page if you want to add one for yourself.


5. Process orders

When something gets paid, hit the Paid button to mark it Paid. When an order's ready, hit Ready to mark it Ready and call the customer's name.

The button in the footer gives you a summary of everything still to make — useful when the queue is busy and you want a clean checklist.


6. Scan products at the counter

Some products don't need to be prepared, just sold — cans, snacks, anything pre-packaged. For those, use the Scanner button in the footer.

Your browser will ask for camera permission. Accept, point the camera at the barcode, and the product gets added to the queue automatically. Scanned items are marked with instead of a user icon.

No barcode? The search bar above the scanner lets you add a product by name. Misclicked something? The Remove button on the right of a scanned order takes it back out.


7. Wrap up

Orders stop coming in automatically when the end time hits or the max capacity is reached. Need more time or more headroom? Use +5 to bump the cap or +5 to push the end time.

Once everything's paid and picked up, finalize the shift with the Finalize button in the footer. That closes it permanently — you can't edit it after. Make sure every order is Paid and Ready before you do.

For deeper edits (advanced settings, retroactive fixes) use the button in the footer — only shown if you have the extra permissions.

Thanks for baking.
